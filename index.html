welcome to my portfolio

I am Ezekiel Cary, a junior web developer at Mississippi Coding Academies' Jackson campus. Call me Zeek, though. I currently know html, css and Bootstrap. I'm a quick learner willing to try out any new language if it's for the sake of a client who pays right. That said, I will not work on any projects that I find unethical. Just don't ask me to come up with any designs by myself. I don't do aesthetics very well, I prefer to translate other peoples' designs into functioning code. I work quickly and efficiently and I don't take unnecessary liberties.

I look forward to working with you.

who am I?

I've been broke my whole life, doing everything I can to help my single mother take care of me and my brother. School was always a nightmare. Social anxiety ate away at me and I never felt stimulated by any of the things they wanted me to do. I felt like I already knew it and I had no desire to show up. Combined with having to constantly move due to rent getting too high, I was unable to finish. This ended with me, my mother and a bunch of teachers in a room where they told us that they weren't equipped for me and that I should pursue homeschooling. My mother wanted to do whatever she could for me but she was working two jobs and could not do what was needed, so that didn't work either. I've recently obtained my GED with relative ease. Finally, I've found my opportunity to pay back what everyone has done for me. I'm eager to get started working with anyone who's interested. My goal is to become a fullstack web developer and make enough money to support my dear family, as well as my boring hobbies. Preferably from home so that I don't have to be away from my girlfriend for too long at a time. I prefer to keep to myself but I also consider myself good at socializing. I read a lot of manga, I enjoy narrative analysis and I write my own webnovel, with art by my girlfriend. Since I was a kid, I've been messing with computer code. If you know about M.U.G.E.N, that was where my interest was born. I liked video games a lot and the idea of being able to make my own with people from around the world got me into problem solving through changing lines of code. It was annoying, but it was always rewarding when it worked out. I had a knack for finding solutions.
